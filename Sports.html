Sports

Gymnastics

Gymnastics is a collection of exercises that test strength, flexibility, control, and balance. There are two main types of gymnastics, artistic and rhythmic, both of which may be performed individually or in a team. In artistic events, gymnasts perform skilful routines using a large piece of equipment, called apparatus, such as fixed bars, hanging rings, or a raised beam. There is also an artistic event, called floor exercise, that takes place on a springy floor. In most rhythmic events, gymnasts use smaller equipment, or hand-held apparatus, to perform a series of elegant exercises to music. At competition level, judges award points, usually up to a maximum of 10. The gymnast or team with the highest score in each event comes first.

Baseball

Baseball is the national sport of the United States. It is similar to older bat-and-ball games, such as the English game rounders. Baseball matches are played between two teams of nine players each. A match is divided into nine periods, called innings. While one team bats, the other team fields, which involves collecting the ball after the batter has hit it and trying to get him or her out. The baseball field has four bases. The batters have to hit the ball and run between the bases to score points, called runs, for their team. The fielders try to get the batters out in a number of different ways. These could include catching the ball, and “tagging” a runner with the ball as he or she runs between bases. If a batter manages to run around all the bases after hitting the ball without being caught out, he or she scores a home run.

Basketball

Basketball is a fast-paced sport played between two teams. To score points, players shoot a ball through high hoops, which are located at either end of the court. Players move the ball around the court by throwing, or passing, it to a teammate. They can also move the ball down the court by bouncing it while running. This is called dribbling. If the players do not bounce the ball while running with it, they can be penalized for “travelling”. Basketball is played in many countries around the world and is very popular in the United States.

Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played on an oval, grassy ground. In the middle is a narrow strip, called the pitch, with wooden wickets at either end. A cricket match is played between two teams of 11 players each. When one team bats, the other team fields, or bowls, which involves collecting the ball after the batter has hit it and trying to get him or her out. A team’s turn to bat, or bowl, is called an innings. A batter stands in front of the wicket and score runs by hitting the ball, thrown by a bowler from the fielding team, and running between the wickets. If the batter hits the ball to or over the boundary (edge of the ground), he or she automatically scores four or six runs. A match can have several innings, with teams taking turns to bat and field. The team that scores the most runs, wins.

Skiing

Skiing is a popular winter sport and leisure activity. Skiers travel smoothly over snow by using a pair of long, narrow runners called skis, which are attached to special ski boots on their feet. They also use thin, metal poles to help them keep their balance. In competitive skiing, skiers take part in various kinds of events. These include alpine skiing, cross-country skiing, ski jumping, and freestyling. In alpine skiing, skiers race downhill, trying to pass through a series of gates in the fastest time. In cross-country skiing, skiers compete in long races that can be 50km (31 miles) or more. In ski jumping, competitors ski down a raised, steep slope, called a takeoff ramp, reaching high speeds before launching themselves off the end and “flying” through the air as far as possible. In freestyle events, competitors ski down slopes or ramps and perform tricks, turns, and spins.

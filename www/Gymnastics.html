Gymnastics

Gymnastics is a collection of exercises that test strength, flexibility, control, and balance. There are two main types of gymnastics, artistic and rhythmic, both of which may be performed individually or in a team. In artistic events, gymnasts perform skilful routines using a large piece of equipment, called apparatus, such as fixed bars, hanging rings, or a raised beam. There is also an artistic event, called floor exercise, that takes place on a springy floor. In most rhythmic events, gymnasts use smaller equipment, or hand-held apparatus, to perform a series of elegant exercises to music. At competition level, judges award points, usually up to a maximum of 10. The gymnast or team with the highest score in each event comes first.

14-year-old Romanian gymnast Nadia Comăneci was the first person ever to be awarded the maximum score of 10 at an Olympic Games, in 1976.

Gymnasts perform either barefoot or in very thin, soft slippers.

Rhythmic gymnasts perform with a piece of hand-held apparatus, such as a ball, hoop, or ribbon, or freehand, without any apparatus. Performing with a ball involves keeping it continually moving without holding it. The gymnast keeps the ball moving by bouncing, tossing, and rolling it around her body and on the floor.

Gymnasts need to wear a comfortable costume that allows the body to move and twist freely. In team events, the members of the same team must wear identical costumes.

A bouncy rubber ball is one of the pieces of hand-held apparatus used in rhythmic gymnastics. It is 18–20cm (7–8in) across.

The gymnast’s hair must be neatly fastened back, to keep it away from her face.
